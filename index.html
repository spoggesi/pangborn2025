About Me

I am a sensory and analytical scientist with a background in oenology, viticulture, and food engineering. My academic path—rooted in a dual bachelor’s and master’s degree in oenology—has evolved into a multidisciplinary research career spanning molecular biology, analytical chemistry, biosensor development, and sensory analysis.

During my MSc thesis at the Université de Technologie de Troyes, I contributed to the development of two biosensors for the detection of Brettanomyces bruxellensis in wine, through a collaborative project involving nanotechnology and applied physics. This experience led me to pursue a PhD in Food Engineering and Biotechnology at the Oenolab laboratory, under the supervision of Prof. Emanuele Boselli. My doctoral research focused on advanced chemical and sensory characterisation of wine using techniques such as GC×GC-TOF-MS, HPLC-DAD/MS, and sensory panel evaluation.

Since 2022, I have been a Research Officer at the Food Experience and Sensory Testing (Feast) Laboratory, Massey University. Here, I lead projects in collaboration with industry and research partners across Aotearoa-New Zealand, supporting food innovation through consumer-centric product development and sensory evaluation.

My research also explores immersive sensory environments. I am actively integrating augmented and virtual reality technologies—such as sensory immersion rooms and VR headsets—to study food perception in context-rich scenarios. These tools enhance our understanding of how consumers experience food in everyday settings.

My expertise lies at the intersection of sensory science, analytical chemistry, and food technology. I am passionate about applying scientific rigour to address practical challenges in food innovation, consumer well-being, and sensory evaluation.

Research Interests
